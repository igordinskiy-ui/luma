# Luma support catalogue v2

This document describes the implementation contract for the narrow beta. The
copy remains general behavioural self-help and is not medical advice. The
catalogue is deliberately marked `v2-draft` until medical, content, and legal
review approves its digest.

## Routing

The support flow asks for two facts before suggesting an action:

- one of 14 user-selected contexts;
- craving intensity from 1 to 10.

Intensity is presented in three modes: a short replacement at 1–3, a guided
pause at 4–7, and a strong-craving plan at 8–10. Technique ordering combines
context match, intensity range, and the user's previous explicit “helped”
answers. Repeated “same/worse” outcomes lower a technique's priority, but never
hide it: the user keeps the full choice and sees why it was moved lower.

## Interactive support

There are 12 techniques. Each has a timer, at least three concrete steps,
applicable contexts, and an intensity range. Breathing has a reduced-motion
aware visual rhythm. Every run ends with a second 1–10 rating and one explicit
outcome: helped, unchanged, or worse. An unchanged/worse result offers another
technique without closing the session; high remaining intensity also prompts a
change of space and contact with a trusted person.

The combinations of context, intensity mode, and technique provide more than
the 20–30 crisis routes required for beta without filling the product with
generic motivational quotes.

## Returning after smoking

The recovery flow distinguishes six states: one cigarette, smoking for a day,
smoking for several days, fear of restarting, anger at oneself, and
hopelessness. The selected state is stored with the event and determines the
three-step recovery card. The quit history and best period remain intact.

## Adaptation and weekly review

Completed support outcomes personalize future technique ordering without
removing choices. Recent user-selected triggers continue to drive
the dashboard's next-step prompt. The journal's seven-day filter and neutral
summary provide the beta weekly review: total events, completed pauses, and
the most frequent context, with an explicit warning when data is insufficient.

No diagnosis, addiction score, relapse probability, or treatment claim is
derived from these data.
